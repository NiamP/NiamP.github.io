The Growth of ESG Investment and its Impact

Aims

This project explores the growth and impact of ESG investment practices on markets, companies and the wider world. The rise in global temperatures and the increasing effects of climate change have prompted investors to prioritise investments that align with ESG values. Over the past decade ESG investing has become a key focus for capital allocators, which has led to discussion around its effectiveness and whether sustainability comes at the detriment of profit. This project examines the evolution of ESG investing, tries to find correlation between ESG and company performance metrics and evaluates whether an increasing emphasis on ESG has actually had any tangible impact on the world.

Data And Analytical Challenges

The largest challenge in collecting the data for this project was the issue of paywalls. Much of the useful ESG data that exists is proprietary and used by institutional investors at banks and hedge funds, making it inaccessible for me working on a university project. To get around this, I made use of free data sources such as Yahoo Finance using the yfinance API, as well as data from Statista, the Science based targets monitoring report and our world in data API. However these sources presented their own challenges, especially collecting company data from Yahoo Finance, where some companies had available data whilst others did not, which required fetching a huge pool of data and then filtering out companies that didn't have available metrics before applying the data to my visualisations.

Two visualisations, the map and the box plot, were the most analytically challenging due to the significant data preprocessing required. For the map, gathering the data into chunks for each year and removing invalid data for things like continents and other subgroups that the our world in data API contains was the initial challenge. Then learning how to use the Plotly library and learning how to set up the animation and custom scale required some analysis of the data to see what a sensible colour scheme and scale would be. Similarly, the box plot required some use of pandas to calculate the median, upper and lower quartiles for each sector, and to remove entries for sectors which didnâ€™t meet minimum criteria.

The data sourced from the yfinance API also required some pre-processing, with the yfinance API not offering country of origin as a part of its service, I was required to download and use a dictionary relating market indexes to their country of origin, this allowed me to pair the market that a company or fund was traded on to see what country it was based in. I also used Vega-Lite to perform some of the analysis, using the built in Loess regression on my data within the Vega editor.

Automation

I aimed to use APIs as much as possible in this project, as these allow for reusability. For some of the visualisations, including the Top 5 Mutual funds graph, scatter plot and regression plot I made use of GitHub actions, and so at midnight each day my python script which fetches the data for these plots is run and the data displayed on the visualisations on the site is updated. This means that for the more volatile data, such as market caps and prices, the website will remain accurate and relevant at all times.

Conclusion

The visualisations demonstrate that ESG investing and practices are growing in importance, with the value of assets in ESG ETFs continuing to rise. It is also clear that companies are taking steps to reduce their carbon emissions and align themselves more closely with a sustainable future. While the carbon emissions goals are impressive, the overall impact of these efforts will not be fully realized until a greater proportion of firms are setting these targets.

The map clearly shows that many western developed nationa such as the USA, UK and France have made efforts to reduce their emissions in the past 5 years, countries like China and Russia have continued to increase their Emissions. Therefore in order for any meaningful progress to occur in protecting the environment, these eastern Economies will need to reduce their reliance on fossil fuels and therefore reduce their total Emissions.
