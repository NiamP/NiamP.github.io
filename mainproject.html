The Growth of ESG Investment and its Impact

Aims

This project explores the growth and impact of ESG investment practices on markets, companies and the wider world. The rise in global temperatures and the increasing effects of climate change have prompted investors to prioritise investments that align with ESG values. Over the past decade, ESG investing has become a key focus for capital allocators, which has led to discussion around its effectiveness and whether sustainability comes at the detriment of profit. This project examines the evolution of ESG investing, tries to find a correlation between ESG and company performance metrics and evaluates whether an increasing emphasis on ESG has had any tangible impact on the world.

What Are Sources of ESG Investment?

First, I want to look at where ESG Investment is coming from, and whether there has really been a growth in the value of sustainable investments.

We can see that the US dominates in Sustainable mutual funds which is expected given they have the most funds overall. We can also see that ESG ETFs values surged since 2018, hopefully correlating with some tangible change.

What Are The Returns From This ESG Investment

Now, examining the returns from this ESG investment, I analysed the performance of top ESG mutual funds, and the ESG counterpart to the worlds largest index, the S&P 500.

Whilst we can see that the mutual funds may have underperformed, with 3/5 shut down, if we look at the larger sample, we see that actually, the S&P 500 ESG outperformed the traditional Index. This disproves the claims that ESG investments will never match traditional investments.

Are ESG Performance and Company Performance Mutually Exclusive?

Next, I examine whether there are any patterns between the performance of companies in these portfolios and their ESG scores.

Although we can see that ESG score and market cap aren't correlated, there is a relationship between year-on-year revenue growth and ESG risk score. Although the relationship isn't linear, it does show that companies with poor ESG risk score perform worse than companies in the 10-30 range.

So What Are the Results of This Investment?

Finally, I examine whether any of this investment or emphasis on ESG has had any impact on the world

These graphs present a positive picture. They both suggest an effort is being made to reduce emissions and protect the environment and the map shows that the spike in ESG-related investing aligns with western emissions falling. However, China's huge and rising emissions are still concerning.

Challenges

Gathering data from yahoo finance presented challenges as data often wasnt available which would break my code. Secondly Plotly does not natively support custom keys with animations, which required me to find a workaround to 'trick' plotly into holding the key I wanted throughout the animation without affecting the map.

Conclusion

It is clear from the above analysis that ESG investing has had an influence on the financial landscape, driving an increase in awareness of sustainability in investment. The graphs paint a positive picture showing an increase in ESG related funds, challenging targets being set by companies and positive performance of ESG related companies and investments. However, the true impact of these changes will take years to fully materialise.

While ESG investing is clearly having a positive impact by reducing emissions (especially in the west) its benefits have been largely offset by countries like China, who's emissions are still rising. Therefore, a more global effort will be needed to increase ESG investment and reduce total emissions production worldwide to create any real change
