The Growth of ESG Investment and its Impact

Aims

This project explores the growth and impact of ESG investment practices on markets, companies and the wider world. The rise in global temperatures and the increasing effects of climate change have prompted investors to prioritise investments that align with ESG values. Over the past decade, ESG investing has become a key focus for capital allocators, which has led to discussion around its effectiveness and whether sustainability comes at the detriment of profit. This project examines the evolution of ESG investing, tries to find a correlation between ESG and company performance metrics and evaluates whether an increasing emphasis on ESG has actually had any tangible impact on the world.

Notes:

1. The yfinance API is an API that allows users to fetch financial data from Yahoo Finance

2. Yahoo Finance makes use of ESG scores from Sustainalytics. ESG scores for mutual funds are based on their portfolios. The way the scoring system works means that the lower the value, the lower the unmitigated ESG risks the company poses (i.e the better) so companies with a lower score are essentially more sustainable than companies with a larger score.

Are ESG Performance and Company Performance Mutually Exclusive?

Next, I wanted to examine whether there are any patterns between the actual companies in these portfolios' performance and their ESG score to see whether this idea that companies investing in sustainability are doing so to their detriment.

Although we can see that ESG score and market cap don't seem to be correlated, which could be viewed as positive or negative, depending on your outlook, we can see that there is a relationship between year-on-year revenue growth and ESG risk score. Although the relationship doesn't directly state that a better ESG risk score results in better revenue growth, it does show that companies with extremely poor ESG risk scores are performing worse, which is a good sign that consumers and investors are making a conscious decision to distance themselves from unsustainable companies.

Data And Analytical Challenges

The largest challenge in collecting the data for this project was the issue of paywalls. Much of the useful ESG data that exists is proprietary and used by institutional investors at banks and hedge funds, making it inaccessible for me working on a university project. To get around this, I made use of free data sources such as Yahoo Finance using the yfinance API, as well as data from Statista, the Science-Based Targets Monitoring report and Our World In Data API. However, these sources presented their own challenges, especially collecting company data from Yahoo Finance, where some companies had available ESG data whilst others did not, which required fetching a huge pool of data and then filtering out companies that didn't have available metrics before applying the data to my visualisations. In the case of the Science-Based Targets Monitoring Report and Statista, the data was available only as Excel or CSV download files, so for these sources, I just had to download and modify the data in Python before uploading it to GitHub to use in Vega-Lite.

In terms of data preprocessing, the charts from Yahoo Finance, especially the revenue growth and location charts as well as the box plot of SBTi were the most difficult, as these all required a lot of manipulation in pandas to become useable inside of Vega Lite. In terms of creating the visualisation, the map was the most difficult, as I had very little experience using Plotly and so had to rely on its documentation to allow me to build the animation. Furthermore building the custom key is not native to plotlys map function especially when using animations. Therefore plotly would make the scale of the key dynamic meaning it would change on each animation frame essentially rendering the colours useless, this required me to use Python to include dummy data in my csv which allowed me to essentially 'trick' Plotly into holding a constant key throughout the animation whilst not altering any of the data visible on the map.

The other data sourced from the yfinance API also required some pre-processing, with the yfinance API not offering country of origin as a part of its service, I was required to download and use a dictionary relating market indexes to their country of origin, this allowed me to pair the market that a company or fund was traded on to see what country it was based in. I also used Vega-Lite to perform some of the analysis, using the built-in Loess regression on my data within the Vega editor.

Automation

I aimed to use APIs as much as possible in this project, as these allow for reusability. For some of the visualisations, including the Top 5 Mutual Funds graph, Scatter Plot and Regression Plot I made use of GitHub actions, and so at midnight each day my Python script which fetches the data for these plots runs and the data displayed on the visualisations on the site is updated. This means that for the more volatile data, such as market caps and prices, the website will remain accurate and relevant at all times.

Conclusion

It is clear from the above analysis that ESG investing has had an influence on the financial landscape, driving an increase in awareness of sustainability in investment. The graphs paint a positive picture of an increase in ESG related funds, challenging targets being set by companies and positive performance of ESG related companies and investments. However, the true impact of these changes will take years to be fully materialise.

While ESG investing is clearly having a postive impact on emissions especially in the west, its benefits have been largely offset by countries like china, whos emmissions are still rapidly rising. Therefore a more global effort will be needed in to increase ESG investment and thereby reduce total emissions production worldwide.
